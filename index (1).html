FAQ

Common Questions

Are courses fully online or do I need to come in person? +
Most courses are fully online via live video sessions, giving you the quality of in-person instruction from anywhere in Kenya or abroad. Select courses offer optional hybrid attendance, and exclusive masterclasses are held in our Nairobi studio at specific dates each term.
What ages do you cater to? +
We offer programs for teens aged 10–18 in our Junior and Teen programs, adults of all ages in our professional and hobby courses, and dedicated corporate programs for businesses and organisations. Each program is designed with the specific needs of that audience in mind.
What materials or equipment will I need? +
Each course comes with a detailed materials list. For fine art courses, we provide starter kit recommendations from quality Nairobi suppliers. For digital courses, you'll need a laptop or tablet — we'll advise on minimum specifications. Starter material packages can be arranged through the school for a premium experience.
How are payment and fees structured? +
Course fees vary by programme duration and format. Full fee schedules are shared during your consultation call. We offer instalment plans for multi-term enrolments and corporate invoicing for business clients. All payments are made via M-Pesa, bank transfer, or card through our secure portal.
Do you offer certificates upon completion? +
Yes — every student who completes a course receives a Nairobi School of Art & Design Certificate of Completion. Our Professional and Portfolio courses also include a curated digital portfolio review and letter of recommendation from your instructor to support university or professional applications.
Can we arrange a corporate workshop for our company? +
Absolutely. We design fully bespoke corporate programs — from 2-hour creative team building sessions to multi-day design training retreats. Contact us for a custom proposal, and our corporate coordinator will follow up within 24 hours with options tailored to your team size, goals, and budget.

Our Mission

Where Nairobi's Creative Excellence Begins

The Nairobi School of Art & Design was founded on a singular belief: that world-class creative education should not require leaving Kenya. We built the institution we wished existed — rigorous, intimate, and unapologetically premium.

Our mission is to develop Nairobi's next generation of creative leaders — equipping artists, designers, and creative professionals with skills, confidence, and industry connections to thrive globally.

Our Vision

Nairobi as a Global Creative Capital

We envision Nairobi as a powerhouse of African creative culture — a city whose artists and designers shape global aesthetics. The Nairobi School of Art & Design is building the institutions, talent, and networks that will make this vision real.

By 2027, we aim to have placed graduates in major international studios, universities, and galleries — proving that the finest creative education can originate from East Africa.

Teaching Approach

Our Method

01

Studio Practice First

Every lesson is built around making — not watching. Students produce real work from session one, developing skill through guided practice and iteration.

02

Deep Critique Culture

Our intimate class sizes enable meaningful critique — learning not just from instructors but from peers, developing the critical eye that separates good artists from great ones.

03

Industry-Connected

Guest instructors, gallery visits, and live briefs from real clients ensure students graduate with portfolios and connections that open doors immediately.

Online-First Model

World-Class from Anywhere

Our online-first model means you receive the same quality instruction whether you're in Karen, Mombasa, or Kigali. Live sessions, recorded replays, and direct messaging with your instructor make learning flexible without compromise.
